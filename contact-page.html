Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about working with us

What is the typical engagement process?

Our typical engagement starts with a free 30-minute consultation to understand your needs, followed by a proposal outlining our approach, timeline, and investment. Once approved, we begin with a discovery phase to deeply understand your product, users, and business goals before moving into strategy development and implementation support.

How much does it cost to work with TestLoop Labs?

Our consulting engagements are customized based on your specific needs and goals. Projects typically range from $20,000 for focused engagements to $100,000+ for comprehensive transformations. We also offer ongoing advisory relationships starting at $5,000 per month. Contact us for a custom quote based on your specific requirements.

What size companies do you work with?

We work with companies ranging from funded startups to established enterprises. Our clients typically have at least a functioning product in market and a dedicated product team. We've worked with companies from pre-Series A startups to public companies with thousands of employees.

Do you offer remote consulting?

Yes, we work with clients globally through a combination of video calls, collaborative tools, and occasional on-site visits when needed. Our remote engagements follow the same structured approach as our in-person work, with regular check-ins and deliverables to ensure alignment and progress.

What industries do you specialize in?

We have particular expertise in B2B SaaS, consumer subscription products, marketplaces, and fintech. That said, our methodologies are applicable across industries, and we've successfully worked with companies in healthcare, education, e-commerce, media, and more.

How quickly can we see results?

The timeline for results varies based on your starting point and the complexity of your product. Typically, our clients see initial results within 2-3 months, with more significant transformation happening over 6-12 months. We focus on quick wins early to build momentum while working on longer-term structural changes.
